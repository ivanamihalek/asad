Above is an AlphaFold interpretation of the protein Tmem81 structure, used as a banner for the Nature editorial from Nov 26, 2025, celebrating the fifth anniversary of AlphaFold. The main protagonist of the story is prof. Andrea Pauli, whose several-year project was finally brought to shore thanks to AlphaFold-Multimer (I could not locate a reviewed version of the related publication).

In their work, Pauli's lab established that Tmem81 forms trimers with Izumo1 and Spaca6.

TMEM81

Back to the main protagonist — TMEM81. In distinction to modeling its cousins IZUMO1 and SPACA6, here AF2 is on its own. So it predicts some helices, a small beta strand and an immunoglobulin-like domain. Their relative 3D arrangement is left to our imagination.


But how does AF do as much as that - getting the immunoglobulin-like domain right? After all, marvels a publication contemporary to the one form the Prof. Pauli's group, the sequence identity to the (Ig)-like domains in IZUMO1 and SPACA6 is 16–27% .


As it turns out, there are better ways to look for structural similarity, and if AF is worth its salt, it has figured it out. You can look for a sequence of secondary structure elements corresponding to TMEM81, together with some judicious grouping of amino acids by their physical characteristics and alignment of the structurally important cysteines. There is one server that comes to my mind right away (there may be more) that does just that: I-TASSER. The illustration below shows the idea. For more you will have to run I-TASSER yourself, they do not store the results for very long:


Among else, note the honestly reported crystal structures that the model is based on. Here is also a zoom into the IG-region:


There is even some sequence similarity here that can be forced out of BLAST search by adapting the parameters to short sequence search (I tried).

The similarity is high enough for PyMol to align the TMEM81 ig-like domain to its counterpart in IZUMO1 (try as homework). N.B: Pymol's default algorithm aligns by sequence, not by structural similarity. Here is a somewhat messy rendition of the PyMol superposition of the IG-like domains in the SPACA6 / IZUMO1 /TMEM81 trio. The highlighted pieces of the sequence are enough to guide the alignment.

Bottom line: up to this point AF2 did not "predict" or "fold" anything new that could not have been done with more pedestrian methods. An odd choice to celebrate its fifth birthday.


Coda: To be fair to prof Pauli, his enthusiasm came from the trimerization structure prediction by AlphaFold-Multimer, not from AF3 itself. To be noted, however, his lab's work did not prove that the predicted structure is correct, but that the three proteins very likely form a trimer. So how did AF get that? AF-Multimer is trained on known protein complexes. Ig-like domains are popular among adhesion proteins. Let's return to this question in another post.
